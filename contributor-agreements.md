Non-Disclosure Agreement
 
 
A Non-Disclosure Agreement, is also known as a Confidentiality Agreement or Proprietary Information Agreement, is a legal binding contract that creates a confidential relationship between parties. Then its primary purpose is to protect sensitive information, which is shared for a specific purpose, from being disclosed to unauthorised third parties. However, the main purpose of Non-Disclosure Agreement is to allow the exchange of confidential information while legally obligating the recipient to keep it secret.
 
IP and Attribution Policy
 
An IP policy is a comprehensive framework that governs the ownership, protection, use, and commercialisation of intellectual property created within or for an organisation, project or community. These answer the fundamental questions like, who owns what, and what can be done with it?
 
The main purpose of an IP is to define and manage rights to creations of the mind-inventions, software, designs, content, trademarks, and trade secrets, to avoid disputes, encourage innovation and protect value.
 
An Attribution Policy is a specific set of rules dictating how credit must be given when using, redistributing or building upon someone else work. However, the main source is to ensure creators receive credit for their work, maintaining integrity, reputation and compliance with legal license requirements.
